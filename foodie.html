Come eat with me

Nabe

1325 9th Ave, San Francisco, CA 94122

Nabe is a hidden gem in San Francisco. Although the parking is pretty tragic, Nabe is definitely a must visit. Nabe specialty Japanese shabu hot pot paired with a large variety of sake. When entering the restaurant, what truly stands out is their wall of sake. It is not only beautiful, but it captures what they are truly known for. So if you do decide to eat at Nabe for your next meal, be sure to pair it with some sake. For sure, it will enhance your meal to the next level.

When I visited back in January, I got the Sukiyaki. Sukiyaki is Wagyu beef paired with Shirataki in Warashita broth. If I am being completely honest, I have never tested hotpot of such high quality. The meat alone had the most perfect marbeling. The broth was very sweet and filled with veggies, noodles, and pumpkin. It blended very well together. What was interesting to me, however, was the meat is actually dipped in egg as a sauce. I have never experienced this before, but I definitely recommend it. The best part of this experience though, is that at the very end... the turn your remaining hotpot into porridge. I honestly am so mind-blown, I would 100% recommend this to anybody and everybody. This is for sure my favorite shabu hot pot place.
